About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Scientist in the MEB Section of PLEN, Faculty of Science, University of Copenhagen, Denmark. My research focuses on applying AI-driven bioinformatics and computational biology to investigate and engineer PFAS-degrading enzymes, contributing to sustainable biotechnological solutions for pressing environmental challenges.

My work centres on deep learning–based predictive modelling, including DrLungker (drug activity prediction in lung cancer), LungXAI (RNA-Seq–based lung cancer subtype classification with explainable AI), and DeepEntXAI (drug resistance prediction in Enterobacteriaceae). I bring strong expertise in protein modelling, molecular docking, interaction fingerprints, DFT, WaterMap, molecular dynamics, binding free-energy calculations, and automated pipelines for sequence alignment, evolutionary inference, and phylogenetics. My broader interests include AI-driven drug discovery, comparative genomics, and vaccine target prioritisation, with a focus on translational and precision medicine.

My academic journey began in a small town in India, followed by middle schooling in Lucknow and higher education in New Delhi—where I gained expertise in genomics (WGS, WES), proteomics, and computer-aided drug design at leading institutions—such as ICGEB, IARI, Jamia Hamdard, and SRM University—and now continues through international research in Finland and Denmark.
